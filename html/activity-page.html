Football as a sport in Norway

Football is one of the most popular sports in Norway, with more than 270 000 + active players in the country (numbers from 1. January 2020). With so many players invovled in the sport, it's a great oppurtunity to get to know the rules and how to play, so you can enjoy it even more. Want to expand your knowledge about the rules of football and get to know how to play? Keep reading to learn more about the rules of football and some popular football fields in Oslo.

Why choose to play football?

Football is one of the country's biggest sports with plenty of opportunities for new experiences and interactions. Not only does it help people staying active, but it bring people together. Through sport, it becomes possible for you to make friends and memories for life. Football is all about teamwork, where every player is needed to succeed.

How to play football?

Football is played with two teams on the pitch and depending on the age the size of the pitch will differ. When adults play, the pitch size is 120m on the longest end and 90m on the shortest end. You play with 11 outfield players (10 including the goalkeeper) and play a total of 90 minutes (2x 45 minutes).

What are the rules in football?

Football has a wide range of different rules, some which can be complex to understand especially for those who are new to the sport. Understanding these rules can make football easier to enjoy, from offside to fouls each rule plays a key role in the world of football. Want to learn more about football's regulations?

Suggestions on popular football fields in Oslo

The city of Oslo has a large selection of football pitches that are suitable for both professional players and those who play for fun. Here are four suggestion of football pitches you should visit in Oslo:

KFUM Arena

A well-maintained football field popular among local teams and enthusiasts around the neighborhood.

View on Maps

Bislett Stadium

An iconic stadium known for hosting major events in other sports and local matches.

View on Maps

Ferd Stadium

A modern football pitch suitable for all ages and skill levels.

View on Maps

Differences between football fields in the eastend and westend of Oslo

The football pitches in Oslo vary between the different ends in Oslo. In the west, you will find more new and upkept pitches, often linked to wealthier sports teams and better public funding. In the east, there are more people and fewer inferior fields, which especially affects young people who also want to enjoy football.
